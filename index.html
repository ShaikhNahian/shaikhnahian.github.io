Research

Deep Autoencoder Based Protection for Malware Detection Models from Adversarially Perturbed Data.

Malware detection, a crucial aspect of cybersecurity, is constantly challenged by evolving cyber threats. We introduce a novel approach, training and evaluating various ML and DL classifiers for malware and benign sample classification, and investigating their vulnerabilities to adversarial attacks. We go beyond the conventional by generating adversarial samples using multiple attack methods and measuring their impact on classification performance. Furthermore, this work proposes an approach that leverages autoencoders to enhance classifier robustness against evasion attacks. Encoding data with an Autoencoder and retraining the classifiers improved performance and significantly reduced misclassification rates. The experimental evaluation demonstrated that the DT performed the best after encoding data and retraining classifiers with the fewest false negatives for the Malware class and Benign class. The findings highlight the importance of addressing adversarial attacks and advancing defenses to ensure robust and secure cybersecurity measures in the face of evolving threats.

Authors: Shaikh Nahian, and Mohammed Moshiul Hoque .

Presented at 4th IEEE International Conference on Signal Processing, Information, Communication and Systems 2025 (4th IEEE SPICSCON 2025), November 2025. Proceedings accepted, pending publication in IEEE Xplore. Manuscript available upon request.
